## Clausius-Clayperon Equation 

### Clayperon Relation 

:::{prf:theorem}
At phase boundary, the chemical potentials are equal:
\begin{equation}
\mu(\alpha, p,T)=\mu(\beta, p, T)
\end{equation}
where $\alpha,\beta$ represent different phases
:::
Consider the change in chemical potential during phase change $\alpha\to\beta$
\begin{align*}

\mu(\beta, p,T) - \mu(\alpha, p,T)&= V_m(\beta)dp - S_m(\beta)dT-( V_m(\alpha)dp - S_m(\alpha)dT) \\
(V_m(\beta) - V_m(\alpha))dp &= (S_m(\beta) - S_m(\alpha))T \\

\end{align*}

First we recall what determines **evaporation rate:
:::{prf:proposition}
The evaporation rate is given by:
\begin{equation}
E\approx \rho C_W \|v_{wind}\| (q_s^* - q_s)
\end{equation}
where $q_s$ is specific humidity, $q_s^*$ is the saturation specific humidity
:::
